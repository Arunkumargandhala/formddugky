About DDU-GKY

DDU-GKY, or Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Grameen Kaushalya Yojana, is a skill training and placement program by the Ministry of Rural Development (MoRD) in India. It focuses on skilling rural youth aged 15-35 from poor families, aiming to provide them with jobs having regular monthly wages. The program emphasizes sustainable employment through post-placement tracking, retention, and career progression.
Here's a more detailed breakdown: Target Audience:
DDU-GKY specifically targets poor youth in rural areas, with a mandate to train one-third of trainees as women. Focus: The scheme aims to reduce poverty by providing rural youth with marketable skills and connecting them with job opportunities.
key features:
Demand-driven skill training:
Training is designed to meet the needs of the job market.
Placement-linked:
The program actively assists trainees in finding employment.
Emphasis on post-placement support:
DDU-GKY provides support to ensure trainees remain employed and advance in their careers.
